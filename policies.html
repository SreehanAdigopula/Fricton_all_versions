Friction Desk Notes

Policies

Clear notes about data, accounts, media links, and third-party services inside Friction.

Friendly Version

This is a student-project policy page, not a formal legal document.

Account & Data Policy

  • Saved App Data: Friction may save your sessions, distractions, breaks, completions, pet progress, themes, settings, and media choices.
  • Offline Mode: Offline progress stays in this browser's local storage and may not transfer to other devices.
  • Supabase Sync: When signed in, Friction syncs app progress to Friction's Supabase database for your user account.
  • Minimal Data: The app should only store data needed to run the focus experience.

Login Policy

  • One App Database: Users sign in with an email and password. They do not need to create or connect their own Supabase project.
  • Account Storage: Friction stores each signed-in user's profile, session totals, focus history, settings, and pet progress under that user's account.
  • User-Owned Access: Each signed-in user should only read and update their own focus data.
  • Fallback: If Supabase is unavailable, Offline Mode lets the app keep working locally.

Music & Media Policy

  • Focus Environment: Only one built-in or custom study source should be active at a time.
  • Custom Links: You can save YouTube, YouTube Music, Spotify, or other study media links locally.
  • Third-Party Limits: YouTube and Spotify may block embeds, require sign-in, show previews, or change playback behavior.
  • Appropriate Use: Custom media should be safe, study-friendly, and not used to harass or mislead others.

Privacy & Reliability Policy

  • No Selling Data: Friction should not sell focus data or personal progress information.
  • Current Time: The app may use an online time API, but should fall back to the device clock if the API fails.
  • Browser Storage: Clearing browser data may remove offline progress and saved settings.
  • Future Controls: A future version should include clearer export, delete, and reset account data controls.
